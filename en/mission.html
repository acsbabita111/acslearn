📜 ACS Global South Charter

Knowledge Without Barriers · Skills With Dignity · Prosperity For All
🕊️ Preamble

Applied Computer School (ACS) believes that quality education, practical skills, entrepreneurship, and digital opportunity are not a privilege for the few, but a fundamental right of every person. Across many developing nations — particularly the Global South — hundreds of millions of people are denied quality education because of language, economic circumstance, geographic distance, and the digital divide.

ACS is resolved to use modern technology, artificial intelligence, open knowledge, and the power of local languages to break down these barriers, and to enable every learner to move toward a life of dignity and self-reliance.

ACS believes that the purpose of education is not employment alone, but also the building of responsible citizens, capable entrepreneurs, and a knowledge-rich society.

⭐ Core Principles

1
Humanity First
The dignity of every human being comes above technology in every decision.
2
Language Equality
No learner will ever be denied education because of language alone.
3
Skills Above All
Certificates matter, but real skill and employability matter even more.
4
Transparency
Fees, exams, and administration — every process will be clear and accountable.
5
Responsible AI
Technology assists humans, it never replaces them; its use will always be transparent.
6
Good Governance
Every officer, employee, franchisee, and partner will act with honesty, accountability, and lawfulness.
7
Lifelong Learning
Education does not end with a degree.
8
Knowledge Sovereignty
Every society has the right to access and develop knowledge according to its own language and culture.
9
Global South Collaboration
ACS will promote knowledge-sharing among developing nations, honoring local communities and languages while connecting them to global opportunity.
10
Future Generations
ACS's decisions will be made with long-term public interest in mind.

🏛️ Institutional Commitments

🤝 Knowledge Sharing
Respect for open knowledge, proper attribution, intellectual property, and copyright.
🔐 Digital Rights
Privacy, data security, fair assessment, transparent certification, and accessibility.

No bribery, no fake certificates or franchises, complete financial transparency.
🔬 Academic Freedom
Respect for facts, scientific reasoning, and intellectual freedom; no compromise on quality under any pressure.
🌱 Environmental Responsibility
Judicious use of resources in all operations.
💡 Innovation
Continuous encouragement of research and experimentation.
🖋️ Founder Stewardship

ACS is not an extension of any political party, religious organization, business group, or ideological campaign. The organization's primary purpose is education, skills, research, and social development. This institution belongs to no single person — it operates under this Charter and the Master Instruction. The Founder, and the highest administrative body duly authorized under this Charter, are its Stewards, responsible for protecting and continuing its core principles, and accountable to this Charter.

🎯 Public Benefit

In all its decisions, ACS will strive to prioritize the public interest, the interest of learners, and long-term social impact ahead of private gain.

⚖️ Status of this Charter

This Charter is the supreme document of ACS's values. ACS's technical and operational policies are built in guidance from it. In any conflict relating to values, this Charter shall prevail.

📐 Amendment Principle

This Charter may be amended only through a duly approved process, and only when the proposed change does not weaken core principles such as human dignity, freedom of knowledge, transparency, good governance, inclusion, and the empowerment of the Global South. No amendment that abolishes or nullifies the basic spirit of this Charter shall be valid.

🕯️ Closing Declaration

This Charter is a declaration of ACS's public, long-term, and institutional commitment to knowledge, human dignity, equal opportunity, responsible technology, and global cooperation.

📖 About Us — The ACS Story

8 February 2011. In a remote corner of rural Bihar, in Vidyarthi Nagar, Chautham, Khagaria, a dream began.

The goal was to teach children from poor families how to use computers — but at a fee so low they could pay it themselves. ₹5 a month. That's all.

There was no big building, no big budget. There was only one belief — "The children of poor fathers are capable too — they just need a chance."

It was to give exactly that chance that Applied Computer School™ (ACS) was founded.

📅 Our Journey — 2011 to 2036

1
8 February 2011
A start at ₹5/month
Computer education began for the rural children of Khagaria, Bihar, at a rate of ₹5 a month. One centre, one dream.
2
8 February 2017
Partnership with BSDM — a change in direction
With support from the Bihar Skill Development Mission (BSDM), ACS's direction changed. It grew from 1 to 3 Centres. BSDM Code: KYP05030002 ✅
3
2020 — The Covid Years
Founding of FFGPMTrust
When the whole country came to a standstill, the thought arose — there was more to do for these children. In memory of revered grandfather, freedom fighter Shri Gangadhar Prasad, and grandmother Smt. Lakshmi Devi, FFGPMTrust was established. Certificates are now issued in the name of this Trust.
4
2026 — Launch of the Web Portal
80+ languages for the Global South
Launch of the web portal (acslearn.com) offering mobile-friendly courses in 80+ languages for the Global South.
5
2036 — The Goal
500 million students, 80+ languages
Full information on every industry, in every language, in every village in the world. From village to Global South. 🌍

🙏 FFGPMTrust — The Founding Story

🇮🇳
Freedom Fighter Gangadhar Prasad Memorial Trust

The Founder's revered grandfather, freedom fighter Shri Gangadhar Prasad, struggled for the country's independence. Grandmother Smt. Lakshmi Devi stood by his side.

In 2020, during the Covid years, when the world had come to a halt, this Trust was established in their sacred memory.

Purpose: the work our grandfather did for the country's freedom, we do today for the economic freedom of poor children.
