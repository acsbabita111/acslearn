📖 The Beginning

8 February 2011. In the remote villages of Bihar, in Vidyarthinagar of Chautham, Khagaria, a dream began.

The goal was to teach computer skills to children from poor families — at a fee they could actually afford. Rs.5 per month. Just that.

No big building, no big budget. Just one belief — "Children of poor families are capable too — they just need an opportunity."

To provide this opportunity, Applied Computer School (ACS) was established.

🙏 FFGPMTrust — The Foundation Story
🇮🇳
Freedom Fighter Gangadhar Prasad Memorial Trust

The ACS Founder's grandfather, Freedom Fighter Late Shri Gangadhar Prasad, fought for India's independence. Late Smt. Lakshmi Devi stood by his side throughout.

During the COVID-19 pandemic of 2020, when the world had stopped, this Trust was established in their sacred memory.

Purpose: What grandfather did for the country's freedom, we do today for the economic freedom of poor children.

🕊️ Preamble

Applied Computer School™ (ACS) believes that quality education, practical skills and the opportunity to grow are not a privilege — they are every human being's fundamental right.

Across the Global South, millions of people are denied quality education simply because of language, money, distance or lack of digital access. ACS is committed to breaking these walls — using modern technology, Artificial Intelligence (AI), open knowledge and the power of local languages.

So that every learner can reach a dignified livelihood and self-reliance — from L1 (Rs.0) to L15.

⭐ Our 11 Core Principles
1
Human First
In every decision, the human being and their dignity come before technology.
2
Language Equality
No learner is denied education because of language — every content first in its original language, then English, then all other languages.
3
Skills Before Certificates
Certificates matter, but real skills and employability matter even more.
4
Transparency
Fees, exams, certificates, evaluation — every process open and accountable. Fees are never hidden.
5
Responsible Use of Technology
AI is a helper of humans, not a replacement. The final responsibility always remains human.
6
Knowledge for Society
Knowledge is not only for jobs — it is also for society, the environment and human welfare.
7
Local to Global
Respect for your village, your language and your local industry — and a path from there to global opportunities.
8
Good Governance
Every official, employee, Centre and partner works with honesty, accountability and respect for the law. No place for discrimination, corruption or exploitation.
9
Lifelong Learning
Education does not end with a degree — continuous learning is the greatest strength of the future.
10
Global South Collaboration
Promoting knowledge, skills, innovation and digital cooperation among developing nations — for shared progress.
11
Knowledge Sovereignty
Every society has the right to access and develop knowledge in its own language, culture and needs. Local knowledge and global science must complement each other. This is what makes ACS truly different.
🤝 Global South Commitment

ACS is committed to building a human-centred, inclusive and technology-driven education system — offering multilingual, accessible, affordable and quality education, skill development and entrepreneurship opportunities to every country and community of the Global South. ACS will follow the principles of knowledge-sharing, digital cooperation, respect for local languages, innovation and transparent governance.

🖋️ Founder Commitment

ACS is not an extension of any political party, ideology or business group. Our only purpose is to make the people of the Global South self-reliant through human-centred education, technological innovation and social responsibility. — Founder, Applied Computer School™
